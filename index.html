2018 Offseason

With the playoffs behind them, the Raptors and their fans were at a loss for what comes next. Do they keep the core of the team that had the best record in the Eastern Conference? Do they rebuild? A lot of soul-searching until the next season starts in mid-October.

Blockbuster Deal!

On July 18, the Toronto Raptors announced that they were trading away one of the team's most beloved stars, DeMar DeRozan, to San Antonio for Kawhi Leonard.

More on the Leonard-DeRozan Trade

DeMar DeRozan was unlike many of the franchise players who came before him with the Toronto Raptors. For years, Canada‚Äôs lone NBA franchise was jilted by stars who eventually either wanted out or declined to put down long-term roots.

Not DeRozan, who once famously said "I am Toronto."

The city loved him for it, and for the way he helped the Raptors become a perennial contender in the Eastern Conference. But despite nine impressive seasons in Toronto, DeRozan could never get the team over the hump.

The Raptors are taking a significant risk with the trade. Masai Ujiri, Raptors president of basketball operations, is making the deal with a determination that he can convince Leonard to re-sign with the Raptors next summer in free agency. Leonard has been clear that he plans to sign with the Los Angeles Lakers in free agency. If the Raptors can't sign Leonard, they may have given up years of future success that DeRozan may have offered.
